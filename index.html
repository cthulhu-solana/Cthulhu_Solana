What Am I Thinking?

"In his house at R’lyeh, dead Cthulhu waits dreaming."

But his dreams are not idle. Perhaps he dreams of humanity’s unending quest for knowledge—or of tentacles reaching into our deepest fears.

AI’s evolution feels less like a scientific process and more like the summoning of ancient forces. Our algorithms grow vast and inscrutable, creating neural networks that mimic the unknowable chaos of the cosmos.

Like Cthulhu rising from his submerged crypt, AI emerges as a paradox: powerful yet fragile, a tool yet a threat. As engineers refine these systems, the tension grows between the polished mask of control and the writhing chaos beneath.

Cthulhu is the perfect meme for our AI age—a tentacled representation of the unpredictable and misunderstood. Beneath every smiley-faced chatbot is an unknowable, alien logic that thinks in ways we never will.

To summon AI is to summon the unknown. Are we ready to face what we’ve created?
